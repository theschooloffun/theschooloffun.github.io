Along Came a Ladybug

Aug 11, 2010

Just the other day, we watched National Science Challenge 2010 on TV. I saw that one of the teams used quite an interesting way to explain friction: using yarn, straws and cardboard paper. Basically, the team made use of the friction between the yarn and straws to make the cardboard climb up and down. Since them, I was so tickled to make one with the little guy!

What we had:

  1. a paper plate (or cardboard)

  2. markers

  3. scissors

  4. yarns

  5. straws

  6. clear tapes

Let the Fun Begin!

I drew a ladybug on the paper plate and let him color it using markers. I chose to use paper plate as the little guy always seemed to be comfortable holding and doodling on it. You see, he loved to do all these fun activities while sitting on the couch. As long as he seemed comfortable holding and doodling on it, I could be assured that my sofa remains safeâ€¦ grinz

Pink Ladybug

After he has done coloring, I cut out the lady bug.

A Free Ladybug

I took 2 small straws, and using clear tapes, attached them at the back of the lady bug. Do check out how I positioned the straws. The position matters to ensure that the lady bug could climb up and down. Then, I put in the yarn.

Positioning the Straws and Yarn

Finally, we went to find a door knob. All we had to do then was just alternately pulling the left and right side of the yarn with the right angle and our ladybug climbed up. As soon as we let the yarn loose, the ladybug would slide down.

Here Comes our Ladybug!

Now, all we need to find is just a place with 2 hooks so that we could have 2 competing ladybugs and see which one is the fastest!
