Creepy Caterpillar

Aug 26, 2010

Say, who doesn’t like Eric Carle’s famous book, The Very Hungry Caterpillar? Both the little guy and I love this book, even though we don’t really own one.

I think Eric Carle simply knows how to win children’s hearts through his choices of colors. And don’t forget about all those fruits! One apple, two plums, and so on… it is just the perfect book to accompany the little guy while having his meal. I know, not a very good practice, but well… it works!

Since we still have some egg cartons, we thought it would be nice to have a very hungry caterpillar that we could call our own.

What we had:

  1. egg cartons

  2. paint

  3. paint brush

  4. white paper (for the eyes, or you could also get some wiggle eyes)

  5. markers

  6. pipe cleaners

Getting Ready

Let the Fun Begin!

First, we cut out 4 egg cartons to paint.

Next was to color the egg cartons. The little guy already knew what to do the moment he saw the paint brush.

Starting Off with Green

We didn’t use palette this time round. We used baking tray to put our paint. When the little guy happily swept his brush to color his caterpillar, I was so excited to see another caterpillar on the tray… :)

Caterpillar on a Tray

Of course, to this little guy, painting would not be fun if nothing ended on your fingers, hands or even face!

Face Painting

Okie, I admit it. He actually only did the green one on his cheek. I was responsible for that red nose… grinz

Still, I say he loved how he looked then!

Loving it!

Once we were done coloring, I helped him put on the eyes and antennae. This how our caterpillar looks:

Creepy Caterpillar

There were some leftover on the baking tray. So, we took some papers to stamp on it. Check out what we’ve got on our first and second attempts!

A Very Abstract Caterpillar

An Even More Abstract Caterpillar

Wheee… Now we have a creepy caterpillar and 2 abstract caterpillars! We love all our caterpillars!

Obviously, none of them was hungry. But we surely were hungry after all the hard work!
