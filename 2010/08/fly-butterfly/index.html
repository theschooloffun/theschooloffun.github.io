Butterfly in the House

Aug 23, 2010

Bah-teh-flai… bah-teh-flai…

It’s kinda difficult to encourage the little guy to say butterfly when he doesn’t see butterflies that often. Back when I was a kid, it wasn’t that tough to find butterfly. Just step outside and chances are you would get to see at least one within less than 5 minutes.There were even times when I kept caterpillars to observe the whole metamorphosis (uhm, except that I usually missed the last part.. :P butterfly flies… what do you expect?!)

Occasionally, we do get to see butterflies here. Not enough, though. We’ve got to count on butterfly parks to see butterflies frequent enough…

Hey, that calls for a craft-making time!

What we had:

  1. paper plate (for the wings)

  2. marker

  3. scissors

  4. food coloring

  5. self-raising flour

  6. salt

  7. water

  8. toilet roll

  9. brochures/newspaper (to decorate)

  10. paint

Let the Fun Begin!

First, we prepared our puffy paint. We mixed equal amount of  self-raising flour and salt. Then we added some water to make it into paste. Using a few little drops of food coloring, we made several colors to play with.

We also drew 2 curves on the paper plate.

Getting ready

In case you’re wondering why we had another paper with a picture of butterfly. Well, it was more to get him used to painting with puffy paint. Trust me, for a boy who kept saying “Eekss… “ whenever he dipped his fingers inside, that was necessary! It also served well to distract him when I needed to cut the paper plate later on.

Sticky paint.. (for now)

After a while, he found that mixing colors was more fun.

Butterfly in my palette

Hehehe.. but that doesn’t mean he didn’t proceed to the next stage, which was to paint the paper plate.

Preparing the wings

No doubt, it was super messy when he was done painting. That explains why I didn’t get to take any pictures. I was worried that he would start hunting the camera with his sticky fingers!

Anyway, basically, after cleaning up, we put the colored paper plate inside the microwave for about a few seconds. Of course, the paint instantly puffed. Nice and dry!

We called it a day after cutting the wings out. The following day, the little guy continued. We glued some torn brochures onto an empty toilet roll. I also helped him to stick the eyes. I was so tempted to use wiggly eyes, but I also didn’t want the wiggly eyes to drop off and end up in the little guy’s mouth.

Getting ready for part 2

We also put on 2 antennas. Now we’ve got a butterfly in the house!
