Gone Fishin'

Aug 30, 2010

Obviously, we were still into fishes. Another rainy day, another fish game to play… :)

What we had:

  1. colored papers

  2. marker (to draw the eyes)

  3. scissors

  4. double tape

  5. magnet

  6. chopsticks

  7. yarn

Red Flag:

I might not consider starting this project with the little guy if I thought he might be interested to poke himself or anyone with chopsticks.

Let the Fun Begin!

First, we drew a fish on a colored paper.

One fish...

I cut it out and used it as a template to cut out more fishes using other colored papers.

The more fishes, the merrier

We attached magnet behind each fish using double tape.

Magnet on our fish

We then prepared our fishing rod using chopstick, yarn, double tape and magnet.

The fishing rod

It didn’t take a long time to get the little guy into fishing. He truly enjoyed catching and throwing the fishes back.

Catch...

.. and throw back

He kept fishing every now and then. It’s one great pastime to him! Sometimes we put the fishes on a tray, pretending that it was a pond.

Our little pond

We also learned that it was great to make lots of fishes. Sometimes, the little guy could turn murderous. Instead of throwing the fish back, he would just tear it into pieces… :S

Was a fish...
