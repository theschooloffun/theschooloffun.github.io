Ladybugs in Our Garden

Aug 23, 2010

Upon finding out the graffiti art, I knew that taking away his crayon would not help. Instead, I gave him a piece of paper. I taught him how to color sideways, i.e. using the side, rather than the tip of the crayon. To him, it was a novelty. So, he spared me from his tantrum. Phewwww…. :)

The Art of Distraction

Now… what crosses your mind when you saw that picture? Me, I saw a patch of grass! Just need some bugs to make it more colorful!

What we had:

  1. red paper

  2. scissors

  3. glue

  4. white paper (for the eyes, or you could also get some wiggle eyes)

  5. markers

  6. bottle caps

Let the Fun Begin!

First, we cut out the wings from the red paper. Then, we glued them on the bottle caps.

Unidentified Bugs

Then, while fervently praying that the little guy would not decide to use the marker anywhere else, I taught him how to make the spots on the wings.

Dot.. dot...

I helped him paste the eyes and there you go, ladybugs in our garden!

Ladybugs in Our Garden

Did the markers claim any victims, you ask? Nope. Yayy… :)
