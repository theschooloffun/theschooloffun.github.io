Track Ball Painting

Aug 29, 2010

I don’t know about you, but it’s tough to keep my little guy away from all the gadgets around the house.

At the tender age of 1, he already knew his way to our phones, keyboards, remote controls, and so on. And don’t ever think of fooling him with a broken gadget! We tried giving him an old, broken mobile phone… it only took him a day to get bored of it! I guess the LEDs and all the sound these gadgets made were the selling points.

When my husband brought home his trackball mouse, we both know it may not be a great idea. But, little did he know what happened to it next… _grinz_

What we had:

  1. ball (we used the ball from the trackball mouse!)

  2. paint

  3. baking tray

  4. glue and paper (or stickers)

A ball and a paint

Let the fun begin!

We started it off by putting the paper on a baking tray and putting a few drops of paint on the paper.

By then, the little guy was already very anxious to know what we would be doing with the ball. The ball was already on his hand.

I showed him how we could make a nice paint smear effect by rolling the ball left and right, up and down.

At first, he chose to simply rub the ball across…

Rub-a-dub-dub...

Then, it got more interesting when he knew he could simply lift up the tray and let the ball roll around.

A nice paint smear effect!

When we were done, we cut out some stars from a paper. Like usual, the little guy was excited about using a glue stick.

Sticking stars

We pasted the stars… and here what we have to frame… :)

Ready to frame
