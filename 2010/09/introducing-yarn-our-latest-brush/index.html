Introducing Yarn, Our Latest Brush

Sep 26, 2010

After the abstract Vroom… Vroom Painting that we had the other day, we thought it would be nice to do another abstract painting. This time round the car stayed in the garage. We used yarn, instead… :)

What we had:

  1. drawing paper

  2. paint

  3. yarn

  4. toilet paper roll

  5. paint brush

  6. plastic container (or, palette)

Let the Fun Begin!

We used a plastic container for our palette. For no particular reason. Just for a change… :)

Earlier, I thought I would use water to thin the paints. Too bad, the little guy just didn’t have the patience to wait for me to get the water. He quickly took the yarn and played with it.

The yarn is mine!

I taught him how to dip the yarn and swirl it around our drawing paper. Our yarn was rather stiff, so what he painted looked more like colorful stripes. Still, an abstract.. :)

Painting with yarn

We also took a toilet paper roll and tied the yarn around.

Tie the yarn around the ol' toilet paper roll

We dipped in the toilet paper roll, but we couldn’t get much color. Obviously, there wasn’t much paint left in our palette. So, we brushed paint on it, using a paint brush.

Then, we rolled our toilet paper roll around our drawing paper. Wheee.. ^o^ Check out the trail left behind!

Yarn Yarn Vroom!

It was really fun! I think we’ll do this again next time with thinner paints. I bet it would be fun dripping some paint using yarn, too!
