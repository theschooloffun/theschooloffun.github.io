When Life Throws You Toilet Papers...

Nov 30, 2010

Make some crafts! Especially if you find yourself having too many toilet paper rolls… :P

After leaving our routine art & craft session for a very long time, we return with a vengeance. We simply had so many toilet paper rolls that we could no longer delay making anything out of them. And we couldn’t afford to just stop doing only one…

We drew our first inspiration from this post by Emma Kelly.

What we had:

  1. toilet paper roll

  2. glue

  3. scissors

  4. marker

  5. paint

  6. paint brush

  7. white paper (to make eyes, since we don’t use wiggly eyes)

Let the Fun Begin!

First, we painted the toilet paper roll. I used a clothes peg to make it easier to hold the roll when the little guy painted.

Mixing the colors...

Brush, brush..

Then, we cut out a spiral. We cut the shape of the head and added the eyes _blue eyes! Meet our stylish snake! Ha!_ and a smile.

There you go, our friendly snake…

Stylish Smiley Snake

Like I mentioned earlier, we couldn’t afford stopping at one. So, we moved on to our next project.

I thought it would be great to make some other puppets to accompany our stylish smiley friend.

What we had:

  1. toilet paper rolls

  2. marker

  3. cardboard

  4. scissors

  5. stapler

  6. sticky tapes

Let the Fun Begin!

We, or rather, I, drew a boy and a girl on a not-so-thick, not-so-thin cardboard. This was the part where I wished my hubby was just right next to me so that he could draw a better version…:’( *awww!*

A boy and a girl

Then, I cut them out. Notice that I added a kind of base for each of them.

Cutting out the puppets

I thought coloring them would be nice. But, I guess, I should just leave that task to the little guy when he’s older and able to color them nicely. I didn’t have the heart to let him just anyhow color them… :S

So, the next thing to was to place this puppet around a toilet paper roll. I used a stapler. Yet, after stapling them on the base, I realised that it might not be safe for the little guy. His itchy, tiny fingers might just try to play around with the staples. So, I put some sticky tapes around the staples.

Standing together, at last...

Great! Now we have a snake, a boy and a girl.

Since we have those puppets now, shouldn’t a story-telling session follow? I scratched my head trying to think of a famous fairy tale or story with a snake, a boy and a girl. Yet, the only thing that crossed my mind was: the story of Adam and Eve…. :)

Oh well, the story of Adam and Eve it shall be… We realised something was missing, though: the tree! So, again, we moved on to the next project… Hehehe, told you we were back with a vengeance!

What we had:

  1. cardboard

  2. color papers (for the tree, we use the leftover from this, and for the fruits)

  3. scissors

  4. glue

Let the Fun Begin!

This project was relatively fast to make since we still had some colorful “hands” to use. We just needed to paste two hands on the cardboard (for the trunk).

Hullo, Tree!

The little guy then faithfully pasted the “fruits”, which we cut out from a brochure. Notice that he was multitasking … Every time he pasted one fruit, he would turn and play with his mini portable aquarium for a while.

Adding the fruits...

And finally, our tree of forbidden fruits!

Polka dot tree

And here’s the scene that we made for the story.

The story of Adam and Eve

Okie… now, don’t complain about why our version of Adam and Eve are so well dressed. You see, it’s good to plan ahead here… :P Who knows, we might use the puppets for other stories… ;) _uhm, provided that these puppets survived the wear and tear…_
