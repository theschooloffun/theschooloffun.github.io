Dear Santa

Dec 7, 2010

Did you know that 6 December is St Nicholas’ Day? And did you know that the story of Santa Claus begins with St Nicholas?

When I was a kid, it was usually on this date that we would all be busy writing a letter to Santa Claus, telling him whether we had been good or bad (although chances are he already knows!) and what we really wanted for Christmas.

And now that I’m the grown-up, it’s time for me to pass down the tradition… :)

Yet, before that, I think the little guy needs to know how Santa Claus looks like first. It’s hard to write a letter to someone you never met before, isn’t it?

What we had:

  1. paper plates (2)

  2. red crepe paper

  3. scissors

  4. glue

  5. marker

  6. cotton wool

Getting ready for Santa Claus

Let the Fun Begin!

First, we cut one of the paper plates into a Santa’s hat. We also used the leftover to make one small circle.

The template is ready!

Then, it was time to prepare the crepe paper… :) We cut the crepe paper into small pieces and pasted them onto the surface of the hat.

Gotta love the color!

This was the part that the little guy was supposed to do. Yet, he seemed to be not in the mood to do any pasting yesterday. He was more interested to take a grasp of crepe papers and throw them into the air. Replace those crepe papers with flower petals, and he would remind you of those cute little page boys and flower girls at weddings!  :P

Next was to paste some cotton wool. We only started this part a few hours after we first started our art & craft activity, since the little guy had totally lost his interest.

Ho-ho-hat!

Then, we pasted the hat on the “face”, which is the other paper plate.

We drew eyes and mouth and finally, added the mustache. We didn’t add the beard, however, since the edge of the plate looks like beard… :)

Hullo, Santa Claus!

Okie, we’d better get our letter ready now… ;)
