Give a Toddler Some Beans...

Dec 17, 2010

…and you’ll get at least (almost) half an hour of fun! :)

Of course, not just beans. Throw in a muffin tin or other container to add the elements of fun. Frankly, it’s been some time since I last used my muffin tin as… well, a real muffin tin. Yet, I still think that this muffin tin has proven to be a good investment after all!

Remember The Green Water Project that we just had the other day? It turned out that the little guy still likes playing with the measuring cups and spoons. So, we counted them in… ;)

Bean Here, Bean There Again!

Here how it went: the little guy scooped some beans from the plastic container and dropped them into the muffin tin. He used different spoons each time, but I don’t think he was aware that there was a difference in the number of beans he could spoon with a different spoon yet. He just loved scooping and the “clang, clang” sound every time he dropped the beans into the tin.

Scoop-a-doo-yeaa...

He also added another variation to this activity by including his favorite Chuck the Dump Truck… hee hee… :P

Chuck the Bean Truck

Too bad this activity couldn’t keep the little guy occupied for a long time, like in The Green Water Project. I sensed that it was probably because I interfered too much. You see, I supervised him closely while he was playing with the beans as I was afraid that any of those beans might end inside his nose, ears or mouth. I don’t fancy the idea of rushing the little guy to the emergency room… :P Every time he made a suspicious movement, for example touching his ears, I asked him to show me his hands. I guess that frustrated him after a while… :P

Oh well… better safe than sorry.
