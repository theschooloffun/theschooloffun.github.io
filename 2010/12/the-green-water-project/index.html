The Green Water Project

Dec 16, 2010

Seriously, most of the time, it doesn’t take expensive toys to entertain a toddler. But most of the time, it takes a lot of plan Bs (or Cs, and so on).

Just like the other day when I found out that he wasn’t interested at all in making this Christmas tree… I thought making a Christmas tree together would entertain him. Yet, in fact, he was actually more interested in pulling the card boards apart…>_<

Uh-oh Christmas Tree!

So, since he has been showing interest in playing with glasses and water lately, I let him play while I tried to finish making the Christmas tree… :)

I brought out the big, wide pail and went to find my measuring cups. To make it more interesting, I also dyed the water green. He watched closely as the dye dissolved in water. Trust me, even at this stage, I knew that I had won his heart!

Turning the water green

I put all the measuring cups inside the big pail. That way, there would be less mess to deal with after this activity… :)

Ready to measure

And of course, he didn’t need much instruction to start playing around with those cups…

Water.. here, there and everywhere

For a good thirty minutes, he sat there, moving the water around from one container to another using different measuring cups. Good enough for me to get other things done! Ha! >:D

Things got even more interesting when I let him use a syringe. He giggled and squirted the green water (into the pail, thankfully) … :D

Squirt.. squirt...

Hmm.. maybe I should let him experiment with other colors, too… :D Mixing colors, etc… I’d say, it would be really fun!!!
