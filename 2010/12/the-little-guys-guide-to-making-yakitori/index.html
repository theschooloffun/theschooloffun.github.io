The Little Guy's Guide to Making Yakitori

Dec 13, 2010

Somehow, last week’s dinner menu included satay and yakitori. Both included grilling and most importantly, skewers. On these two different days, the little guy observed me closely as I soaked the skewers in water and threaded the meat pieces onto the skewers. He seemed very interested…

No doubt, given a skewer, he would straight away give me a free demo, showing me that he, too, could do that interesting thing that seemed to keep me occupied, instead of playing with him. Of course, giving him a real skewer is never a good idea. So, I gave him a chopstick and watched him carefully as he played around with it, along with Play Doh.

He was so excited when I gave him “balls” of Play Doh. Chef Little Guy quickly threaded the balls onto the skewer.

Threading... one, two, three...

Of course, the fun didn’t stop there. He then took out all the balls from the skewer and flattened some of them using his hands.

Smash!

Next, he took one of his fave fire truck and pressed it against one of the flattened balls. I think he liked to see the mark left behind by the truck since he then took another car and a figurine to press against another flattened play doh.

Vroom...

When all had been properly “marked”, he took them one by one and yes, threaded them onto the skewer again… :) Apparently, he wanted the yakitori to have a different look from the first yakitori he made! Hee hee… I guess, next time I should give him more than just one skewer and lend him my grill pan!

Yakitori, Anyone?
