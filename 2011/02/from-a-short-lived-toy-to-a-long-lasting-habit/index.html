From a Short-lived Toy to a Long-Lasting Habit

Feb 2, 2011

Been keeping some empty containers for the longest time ever to make a bigger version of this.

One of Thomas' Friends

And since the deadline for the spring cleaning is approaching, I thought I should quickly get it done… >_<

The little guy was rather helpful at first. He even helped me put some glue on the wheels.

The Ever-Helpful Little Guy

And we managed to complete our prototype within a few minutes.

Train-tastic!

Too bad… before I got the chance to little-guy-proof it, the little guy decided to do a kind of reverse engineering.

First, he opened the lid. Oh well, I thought, not so bad…

First Step of Reverse Engineering

Then, to my horror, he started ripping the container off.

Uh oh...

Even after I attempted to put the container back, he still played with it. He asked me for coins and stored them inside… :P

A Really Useful Engine

Oh well… after a while, I gave up. The train was certainly short-lived. I decided to make a hole on the lid and let the guy have the container as his piggy bank… :P

Saving Up

I think he likes it better… :P And I hope it instills saving habits in him… :)
