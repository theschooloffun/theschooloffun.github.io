Hocus Pocus Dino

Feb 21, 2011

I’m afraid we haven’t really got over our latest craze in dinosaurs.

So, again, another dino craft to paint :)

What we had:

  1. food coloring (or paint)

  2. water

  3. white oil pastel

  4. drawing paper

  5. paintbrush and palette

Let the Fun Begin!

First, I drew (or rather, I used a stencil to draw) dinosaurs on a piece of paper. I drew two dinosaurs, actually, just in case one of them get too wet to keep, like what happened in our previous activity.

Now you don't quite see them...

We wanted to have a forest magical scene, so we used blue and green food coloring, mixed with some water.

The beauty in this activity was that it almost seemed as if there was nothing on that piece of paper at first. So, when the little guy started adding some colors using the paintbrush, he got more and more excited revealing more of the dinosaur with each brush stroke.

Now you see them, too!

That paintbrush was surely a magic wand! Ha… :P

This whole thing proved to be pretty entertaining for this dino lover, since it could actually keep him brushing until all was completed… :P
