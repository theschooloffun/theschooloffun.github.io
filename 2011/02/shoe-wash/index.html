Shoe Wash!

Feb 17, 2011

A couple of days ago, the weather seemed to turn warm. Uhm, okie… stop giving me that look… “hot” might be the more appropriate term.

I asked the little guy if he was interested to wash the shoes.

He didn’t reply. But, the sight of a pail full of water made his eyes grow round with excitement… :P

And so, we began our very important lesson in washing shoes.

First, we removed the shoelaces.

Pull here

It wasn’t that easy for his tiny fingers. He really had to struggle even to pull out from just one hole!

Next was the easiest and his most favorite part: brushing the shoes.

Brush! Brush!

After completing this part, I went in to get another pair of shoes to wash. Yet, when I saw what he did to that first pair of shoes, I changed my mind. I decided to just stop at one pair and left it to dry (after pouring the water out, of course!)

Full to the brim

Guess what, it rained that afternoon and the following day! We had to wait a few days before the shoes were dry. I know, that tickled me to quote from the Murphy’s Law, too… :P

When the shoes were completely clean and dry, it was time for another lesson: putting on the shoelaces.

A Difficult Task

That proved to be very difficult for a 2-year-old. He persevered for just the first 5 minutes.

Then, he ran away with the clean, shiny shoes…:)
