Stamped, Sealed, Delivered

Feb 3, 2011

Somehow, each year, our collection of red envelopes grows even though I don’t recall ever buying any set. There is always seem to be a vendor, shop  or bank that gives them out for free.

We don’t really celebrate Chinese New Year. Hence, we don’t really have that many opportunities to bask in the tradition of giving out red envelopes. So, there you go, another explanation of the excess supply.

This year, however, one of them proves to be useful.

The Inspiration

I first drew the stems and twigs, following the picture above… and yup, finally, we get the chance to use the stamp pad that I’ve been keeping for a few months, waiting for the little guy to be ready… :)

Bright Red Stamp

The little guy was surprisingly quite cooperative. He diligently stamped on the places I pointed. He only branched out when we ran out of buds to stamp next to… :P

Here, Mommy?

Still, those prints turn out nice. When we’ve completed our picture, I pasted it on a red construction paper. There you go… just put it inside an envelope, and we’ve got a Chinese New Year card to send… :)

A Limited Edition Chinese New Year Card

Sorry, people… but this card is not available at any major book store… :P

Happy Chinese New Year!
