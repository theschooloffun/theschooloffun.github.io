What Blue and Yellow Make

Feb 19, 2011

I used to just stock up on two types of food coloring: red and green. Yet, but as I idly browsed through the baking product aisle in the supermarket _till now, I still haven’t figured out why I was there in the first place_, I couldn’t help but to bring home blue and yellow. They were on sale!

I, however, still can’t see myself baking anything and coloring it with blue. Although blue is one of my favorite colors, I still find anything not naturally blue too bizarre to eat. Even when there are many kinds of food and beverages in different shades of blue nowadays.

Now that I have blue (plus, yellow!) food coloring at home… hm, maybe I should learn to like it. Or, at least, I should try with light blue first… :)

It’ll take some time… as for now, I shall just leave the food coloring to the little guy to play with.

Like what we did on this piece of kitchen towel…

Little Blue Boy in Action

It did turn out pretty nice and I did want to keep it.

A Short-lived Art Piece

Too bad, the little guy got a little too excited and pour all what we had left in the palette into the tray _I know.. luckily, we used a tray!_.

The kitchen towel was soaking wet. Not in rainbow colors. It looked more like eerily black ink!

I was very worried that he would then try to empty the tray, spilling everything and staining things in our living room. So, I quickly got myself busy cleaning up… that’s why I didn’t bother to take any more pictures… :P

Luckily, except some fingers, nothing else was stained. We could keep the bleach back … :)

Oh well, I guess that was one downside to using food coloring for painting: It’s not really washable!

There… I did warn you… :P
