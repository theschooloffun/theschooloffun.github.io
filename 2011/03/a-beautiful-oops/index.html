A Beautiful Oops!

Mar 22, 2011

Ever since I let the little guy play with a spray bottle, it’s been one of his favorite bath toys. As I am currently letting the little guy experiment with different ways of painting, it just feels right to include that spray bottle … :)

And so I loaded the spray bottle. Not fully, though… just in case he decided to pour the whole thing out, instead of spraying. I used purple paint, thinned with water.

Half-loaded...

For an obvious reason, I chose to do this fun painting outside…>:P I placed the paper on the staircase railings.

I pasted some leaves on the paper, hoping for some silhouettes to admire after we finished spraying… :)

Ready to spray

The little guy, too, was already in his “painting attire”, i.e. never-mind-if-it-gets-dirty-and-stained attire.

In short, we were ready to spray… _grinz_

So, here comes the painter.

Squirt... squirt...

To my surprise, even though the spray left purple dots all over the little guy’s T-shirt, the patterns on the paper barely looked purple! >_<

Plain Water?

I guess I had used too much water to thin the paint. Oops… :(

But, of course, there are certainly no mistakes in art. There is just another opportunity to learn something new… ;)

Once the little guy was done spraying, I took down the paper and went to get some food coloring.

We dripped some food coloring onto the wet paper and watched in awe as the colors blended.

The Real Work of Art

Then, I let the little guy use a skewer to mix colors and draw on the paper.

Just Add a Skewer

Very fun. And very messy… :P

Good thing we did this outside!

In the end, the paper was too wet to keep. So, the picture above was the only thing we have to remind us about this fun experiment.

The little guy, however, had somehow grown a mustache during the process… :P

The Blue Moustache

It disappeared after he took a bath a few hours later. Yet, before that, he certainly had a great laugh looking at himself in the mirror.
