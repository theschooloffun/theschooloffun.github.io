Paint a Forest

Mar 10, 2011

I just found a small, long piece of cardboard recently. It was probably a leftover piece from the cardboard that we used to make our castle.

While it was too small to build into anything, it was just the right size to use as a kind of brush… :)

What we had:

  1. 2 small pieces of cardboard (I cut mine into two shorter pieces)

  2. paint (brown and green)

  3. drawing paper

  4. paint brush (optional)

Getting ready...

Let the Fun Begin!

First, I showed him how to dip the cardboard stick into the brown paint and sweep the paint down on a drawing paper.

Sweep...Tree... Sweep... Tree...

There you go, we’ve got some tree trunks… :)

We then took the other cardboard piece and roll it. We dipped it into the green paint and stamped it on the drawing paper. Just right above our tree trunks.

I actually forgot to take a picture of the rolled cardboard piece before throwing it, but I guess you could roughly tell how it looked from the green stamps that we made… :)

Growing trees...

The little guy found the paintbrush not long after. He then decided to use the paintbrush instead, flickering it here and there…

Stamping brush

He also asked for his beloved kitchen towel roll, which he had been painting for the past few days… :P

(Still) On a Painting Spree...

It wasn’t for long, though… Soon enough, he went back to his trees and completed a forest… :)
