Rainbow Dye

Mar 16, 2011

With five bottles of food coloring sitting at home, I have become very generous when it comes to using them.

Often, I just let the little guy play color mixing.

Indeed, it is one fun activity for him. Yet, often, it could be pretty tiring to monitor him closely. You see, the little guy is never a fan of aprons. Not even bibs. Certainly not a good thing when you know that food coloring is _not _that washable.

Color mixing in progress..

Lately, however, I realize that he’s becoming more and more careful not to spill any on his clothes. That certainly puts my mind at ease… :)

So, one day, after letting the little guy play some color mixing, I took a few pieces of kitchen towel. I folded them a few times and taught the little guy to dip each corner into a color, one at a time.

After dipping...

The folded piece looked something like this…

Wet and Colorful

Not that special, eh?

But when you open it up, it would turn into something nice… :)

Something To Dye For!

The inspiration came from a great book that we recently found  in the library. You should try to find that book, too. It has tons of ideas to keep the little ones busy, especially during school holidays…;)

We managed to paint four pieces of kitchen towel before we ran out of food coloring. One of them, however, was torn when we tried to open it up…:(

So, we only had 2 other designs to show off … :)
