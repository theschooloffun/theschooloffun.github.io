One Happy Tree

Apr 29, 2011

Back when the little guy was into black, one of the other activities that we did was this blow painting.

Blow, blow...Pfft!

We did something similar before, but I thought it would be easier if we used a straw this time round. Yet, it was actually more difficult. Or maybe, we just didn’t get the right consistency of the paint. Not watery enough, perhaps…

So, the little guy really needed a lot of help to make this one. We really had to make use of gravity to get the paint move. We held the paper upside down to make the “trunks”. We only managed to blow for the “branches” and “twigs”.

The Tree

We put it aside to dry and eventually, forgot all about it for weeks… :P

I only remembered about it when we had just finished our effervescent vitamin C. The lid just reminded me of the little guy’s favorite stamps. So, I stuffed in some crumpled clear tapes and we were all ready to “stamp”… :)

Introducing Brush #1

While the little guy loves the stamping part, I love the fun patterns the crumpled clear tapes made… :)

Let the Stamping Begin

I also prepared another “brush”, a scouring pad.

Introducing Brush #2

… and we got more fun patterns (look at the orange ones!) for our tree… ;)

Just Keep Stamping!

And here’s what we got:

A Tree in Bloom

One happy tree… :)

I’ll share about what we used this painting for in another post next time… :)
