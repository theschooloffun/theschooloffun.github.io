Sticky, Sticky Magnet

Apr 1, 2011

The latest series in our various activities to explore different ways of painting involved some paper clips and fridge magnets.

This activity didn’t take long to win his heart. In fact, this seemed to be another favorite of his… :)

What we had:

  1. paint

  2. paper clips (we used 2, but you could certainly use as many as you like!)

  3. fridge magnets (or any other strong magnets)

  4. drawing paper

  5. chairs (or table)

  6. clear tapes

Let the Fun Begin!

I first arranged the two chairs: next to each other, but not too close to each other. The two chairs would serve as a kind of platform for the drawing paper. Then, using clear tapes, I sealed the drawing paper across the two chairs.

Ready to Paint

As you could see from the picture above, I placed the paper clips on top of the paper. We would be using the fridge magnets to move these paper clips from underneath the paper… :)

After putting some paint, of course.

Making Colorful Tracks

As we continued moving the paper clips using the fridge magnets, our artwork slowly appeared. Whohoo…

Check out the Swirls!

It was really fun! Apart from the tiny splatters from the moving paper clips, this was also not as messy as I thought it would be. Since the little guy was only moving one fridge magnet underneath, his fingers were relatively clean! :p

When I thought the little guy had lost interest to continue, I taught him how to peel off the clear tapes so that I could leave the painting outside to dry. He seemed to enjoy peeling off the clear tapes, so I left him on his own to keep the paper clips away.

When I came back, I found him adding some colors to the drawing paper. Not with paper clips, of course. He had somehow invented another way of painting… :P He made a large ball of crumpled clear tapes, dipped it into paint _yeah, he opened the bottle himself.. >_<_ and started stamping.

Introducing Clear Tape Painting

So, once again, the little guy was busy painting. After a while, he asked for the paper clips again.

Hehehe, not bad… since this whole painting activity kept him occupied, we had quite a peaceful morning.

I also love the artwork that we’ve got at the end… :)

An Abstract Painting

I could easily tell that the little guy also loves this painting method. Later that day, after his nap, he pulled out and arranged the chairs, just like how I arranged the chairs that morning.

He then gave me his widest smile and said, “color…?”

Yeah… a signal that he wanted to do it again… :P

I let him use finger paint this time round.

Experimenting with Finger Paint

It was stickier and rather “heavy” to move around, though.

So, after a while, the little guy decided to re-purpose the fridge magnet… :P

He placed the fridge magnet on the paper and started moving it around, just like a sponge…. hee hee…

A Different Kind of Magnet Painting

Now, that was messy! :P
