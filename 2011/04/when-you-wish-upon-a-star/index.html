When You Wish Upon a Star

Apr 5, 2011

Although it has been quite some time since I first introduced the little guy to oil pastels, it is only recently that he seems to like using oil pastels very much.

Smelly, Messy, but Fun

Yes, he still likes to peel the paper. But, he would doodle and scribble majority of the time.

Peeling is Fun, too

Just like with colored pencils, it is easy to tell which color the little guy is into with the oil pastels. Each time, there would be one certain color that he would use more than the others.

Like this one.

Black!

Strangely, he continues to like black for more than a week.

Even though I don’t share the same delight in his choice of color, I should still respect his choice… :) Even when we use other tools to draw.

So, off we went in our quest to draw something black, yet different. We drew the inspiration from one of Eric Carle’s books, Draw Me a Star.

First, we read the book together again, or rather, attempted to read together a couple of pages before he ran away to grab the brush.

Next, we painted the paper in black, blue and white.

Paint the Sky Dark...

We left it to dry overnight.

The next day, we took different paint colors. To make my life easier (read: no meltdown, in case the little guy still insisted on black), I only brought out selected colors…. Hee hee… :P

The little guy then painted the “stars”.

Drawing the Stars...

Easy peasy! :)

Eric Carle's vs. The Little Guy's

Now all we need to do is make some wishes. We have lots of stars to wish upon! ;)
