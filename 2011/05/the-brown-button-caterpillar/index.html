The Brown Button Caterpillar

May 30, 2011

As the little guy gets older, I feel more comfortable leaving him with a brush and bottles of paint.

Hum while You Paint

Every now and then, he would ask me to help him open the bottle (when the lid is stuck, otherwise he would open it himself) and start painting. Most of the time, I can’t really tell what he’s painting, though he seems to try to explain as he goes along… (yup, he still speaks gibberish, and sometimes sings, as he draws… :P)

They all look abstract to me. Just take a look yourself.

Abstract #1

Abstract #2

Abstract #3

But, hey… this painting is an exception!

His Very Hungry Caterpillar

It’s his very own version of Eric Carle’s The Very Hungry Caterpillar!

While the resemblance is probably pure coincidence (I’m not even sure if the little guy would even agree!), I love the feeling of finally figuring out what he painted… hee hee… :p

So, to celebrate, I made him his very own hungry caterpillar to play with. _grinz_ A reddish brown button for the head and a long green ribbon for the body… There you go, his caterpillar is ready to “eat” all the felt fruits, just like in the story. In fact, I used similar shape and the same numbers of fruits for each kind of fruit.

A Very Full Caterpillar

He seems to enjoy putting in all 15 felt fruits one by one. Bwahaha… bet he didn’t realize that it was part of the tricks to get him to practice buttoning!

Gobbling the Plum

I also had the chance to let a 4-year-old try and this caterpillar seemed to work wonders in keeping him quietly occupied. Whoohoo… I guess the little guy wouldn’t outgrow this caterpillar anytime soon then. Now that is what I call a good investment!
