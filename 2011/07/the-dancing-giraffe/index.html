The Dancing Giraffe

Jul 9, 2011

There’s nothing new about this little curious cook aka the little guy that I have at home. He seems to rate my kitchen utensils way above his toy collection. Whatever I do in the kitchen never seems to fail to amaze him.

Like when I cut some potatoes into cubes. There was no way I could escape from letting him try when he was that wide awake…

Cutting Corners...

With a bread knife instead of a proper knife, the little guy just had to cut corners… So, instead of nice cubes, we got some nice_ unidentified shapes… :P_

Too Nice to Throw...

Yes, they were certainly too nice to throw.

Since the little guy recently likes to read Giraffes Can’t Dance by Giles Andreae, I thought we should use those potatoes to make our own giraffe… :)

What we had:

  1. paper

  2. marker

  3. scissors

  4. brown paint

  5. potato pieces

Let the Fun Begin!

All I needed to do was to draw a giraffe on a construction paper. The rest of the steps, well except the cutting, of course, was the little guy’s to complete… :)

Yup, he just needed to dip the potato pieces into the paint and start stamping. That easy!

Stamp!

Fast and easy, in fact. I even had to find another way to entertain him while we waited for it to dry, giving him another episode of Being the Curious Cook.

Making Good Use of His Energy

There was, however, one problem. As the picture of the giraffe was plain at first (i.e. no patches), the little guy insisted that it was a horse, not a giraffe. So, when we finally got our giraffe ready, he kept calling it, “Dancing horse!”

The Dancing Giraffe

Oops! :P
