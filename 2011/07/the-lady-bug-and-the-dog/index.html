The Lady Bug and the Dog

Jul 11, 2011

This is what you nearly miss in my previous post on The Dancing Giraffe…

After we had both decided that our giraffe has had enough patches, we realized we still have some leftover paint. So, I quickly cut out a lady bug for him to do a dot painting on…

The Yellow Lady Bug

I showed him how to use the cotton bud to paint, then I gave it to him.

Dot... Dot...

I watched him closely as he painted, praying that he would not poke anything else with that cotton bud.

Thankfully, he didn’t… He really concentrated on finishing his lady bug, which he called “spider” at first.

Done!

When he’s done with the lady bug, he put down the cotton bud and started to dip his fingers into the paint. I quickly passed him a piece of paper.

Five Fingers Down...

One Dirty Hand

Then, suddenly… he pointed at one part and shouted, “Doggy!”

Doggy!

I must really applaud his imagination… ^_^” It took me some time and a few rounds of turning the paper to figure out what he meant!

Here, let me help you…

Brown Dog, Brown Dog... What do You See?

Too bad he then decided to hide the dog behind the grass…

Camouflaging...
