The Little Guy's Guide to The Rainbow Fish

Jul 20, 2011

There’s always a certain thrill to ransack daddy’s treasure box and find something exciting among your loot.

Like this box full of barely used watercolor paint tubes.

Finders, Keepers! Ours!

After forcefully claiming the whole box as ours, we decided to paint something. We didn’t have to look hard to find an inspiration, especially since The Rainbow Fish is among the books we read for this week.

What we had:

  1. drawing paper

  2. pencil

  3. black marker

  4. scissors

  5. watercolor paints

  6. paint brush

  7. salt

  8. water

  9. cling wrap

Let the Fun Begin!

First, I drew the fish in pencil on the drawing paper.

Ready to Paint

That was about all for my part, except for the outlining later on. The rest of the steps were left for the little guy.

After countless painting sessions, it seems natural that he immediately wanted to take one paint brush and start dabbling on the paper. Luckily, I was fast enough to direct him to paint only with water first. Otherwise, he would miss the part where he could see how different paints blend together making new colors.

Super Strong Brush Stroke

And that, ladies and gentlemen, makes any painting done by even a 2-year-old look like an exquisite work of art… :P

The next step was his favorite: sprinkling salt. The salt would create a nice texture for the scales as it absorbs the water in the paint and make the area beneath it lighter. I realize I shouldn’t have used fine salt, though… The textural effect would certainly have been more obvious if I use coarse salt.

With a Pinch of Salt

The little guy really loved this part so much that he even continued to sprinkle on the “water” painting, which he basically painted in blue using a big brush.

More Salt, Please...

Thankfully, I didn’t pass him one whole bottle of salt. In fact, it was really wise to put some salt in a small container for him to use. He ended up pouring the whole container on this painting… :P

While the blue paint was still wet, I quickly put cling wrap over the wet piece of paper and left it there until the paint dried.

Not Nicely Wrapped...

And we took it off, tadaaa… a nice textural pattern! Just the right pattern for our little fish to swim in… :)

Feel Like Swimming?

Here, let me put the fish inside. I’ve outlined the edges using a black marker and cut it out.

Our Rainbow Fish

Look nice, eh? All the tricks have certainly made it hard to believe that this painting was done by a 2-year-old! And best of all, this 2-year-old really enjoyed himself throughout the process… :)

Oh, by the way, see the sequins there? Yup, we’re ready to re-read The Rainbow Fish and show how a good fish shares its glittering sequins, oops… I mean, scales… :)
