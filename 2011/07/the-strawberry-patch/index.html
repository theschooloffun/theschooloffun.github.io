The Strawberry Patch

Jul 19, 2011

Remember when I told you that 3 out of 10 activities that I planned out for the little guy didn’t turn out as I expected? This painting is one of the examples.

Small and Big

It was meant to be a simple stamping activity using a toilet paper roll and his fingers to make a strawberry picture (See the small strawberry on the left? That was the example I gave him).

The little guy, on the other hand, had his own plan. He used his hands and fingers and painted a bigger strawberry on the right side… >_<

Looking at the final product, I initially didn’t feel like writing about it. After all, it was just another finger painting session – something that we do almost once a week. Trust me, I have a stack of his artworks which I haven’t posted, or don’t intend to post, here…:P

Looking at the horrid colors, I almost sent it to the dustbin, too. I guess the only reason the paper was still in the house is because I have been quite a procrastinator lately.

Looking at it once again, I thought I should give it another chance. At least, I should let it be useful one more time before, if I really decide to, I throw it into the dustbin of history.

So, I cut out some strawberry shapes using red and green construction paper. I let the little guy put some glue on the back and stick them on the painting.

Sticky Berry

I know, it still looks rather plain. But wait till you see what we did next!

We used correction pen to decorate our strawberries. At first, I thought of using a black marker. But I know the marker wouldn’t survive the violent blows the little guy could make… :p The correction pen really seems like the best solution.

Counting the Seeds...

No matter how awful the painting still looks now, it was certainly an interesting way to introduce him to the fact that strawberries have seeds on the outside, not inside. Uhmm… I know I shouldn’t really call them “seeds”, but how could I explain “achene” to a 2-year-old and that strawberries are not really fruits? :P
