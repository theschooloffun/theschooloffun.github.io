When He Wanted to Rock the Boat...

Jul 7, 2011

As wonderful as they look on the photos posted in this blog, I must admit that sometimes, say probably about 3 out of 10, the activities that I planned out for the little guy didn’t turn out as I expected. It could be because of his lack of interest (blame it to the terrible two!) or simply because he had a totally different idea on how to do things (again, blame it to the terrible two!). Even so, most of the time, the outcome could still be considered rather ‘positive’.

There are, however, some that give an outcome that I don’t quite fancy… :(

Like when I taught him how to blow water bubbles through a straw…

Bubbles!

I remember teaching him that before and there was no incident AT ALL. Yet, obviously this time, it just had to be different.

It started off as a lesson on how the wind could move a boat. We had our paper boat ready. So all we needed then was a little bit of gust.

Ah.. there! The little guy huffed and puffed with all his might…

A Hurricane's A-blowing

Then we moved on to learning about waves: observing how the wind causes the waves and how the waves, in turn, would bring the boat up and down.

Too much for a 2-year-old? Most definitely!

It didn’t take him long to figure out that if he pushes the straw deeper instead, he would get bubbles…

Lots and lots of them….

Who Needs Waves when You Have Bubbles?!

And see that cheeky smile?

*gulp*

I knew it! He just took a gulp of the water… Yikes!

Fortunately, it was drinking water. Yet, I never thought that he would even try to suck at the straw…

And just as I tried to recover from the shock, he gave me a long “Ahhhhhh!”… and told me how yummy “the drink” was… Duh!

Ahhhhh!

So, people, the next time you want to try this, make sure you prepare clean drinking water. Just be prepared for the unexpected, will you…
