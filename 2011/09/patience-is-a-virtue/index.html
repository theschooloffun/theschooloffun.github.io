Patience is a Virtue

Sep 2, 2011

That’s exactly one of the things I keep telling myself when this kind of thing happens…

Messy, Mommy!

Especially when the above happened not long after I mopped the floor….^_^” Yup, the floor was nicely clean and dry before the little guy began his “water project”. Seriously, now that he knows how to get a bucket of water on his own, the weather forecasts should probably include regular flood alerts.

Careful, Car!

Thankfully, in the end, I managed to keep my cool. I let him play peacefully and when he had given me the confirmation that he was no longer interested to play, I started to mop the floor (again!).

Of course, while I cleaned up, I had to make sure he would not take sudden interest in getting another bucket of water. I looked around for something interesting that could occupy him for at least for 5 - 10 minutes. Check out what we got.

Pompom! Lots of Them!

Pompoms, a tweezer and a muffin tin! Perfect!

As soon as he learned how to use the tweezer, he carefully picked each pompom and placed it inside the muffin tin.

Frankly, at first I was impressed with his patience and focus. Well, I should… we’re talking about a toddler who could barely sit still for even a minute here!

One by One

For a while, the thought of patting myself on the back crossed my mind. Hey… maybe I finally managed to turn this little guy into a patient mini-me…0:)

Then… he poured out the whole container of pompoms into the muffin tin, clapped for himself and cheekily said “Well done!”

Done, Mommy!

Yup, that’s it. End of story.

Phewww… at least, the floor was already clean and dry by then…
