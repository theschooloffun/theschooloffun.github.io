Blow, Blow Your Boat

Mar 11, 2012

I always find cardboard simple, yet versatile. I started collecting used cardboard, and all of sudden… they keep piling up in one corner! And so the hunt began for cool ways to re-purpose them :)

Cardboard, Cardboard...

What we had:

  1. cardboard

  2. cling wrap

  3. scissors

  4. colorful paper

  5. straw

Let the Fun Begin!

Since the little guy is still very much into water play, I thought getting him involved to make a little boat would be great.

First, he helped me wrap the cardboard with cling wrap. This cardboard would serve as the base of the boat, so I made sure the cling wrap seal tightly around the surface to make it last longer.

Wrap It

It turned out that the little guy was so fascinated with the cling wrap that he only wanted to play with the cling wrap. The boat simply had to wait… :P

The next day, we continued. I taught him how to make a hole in the center of the cardboard using a pair of scissors. Not easy for him… but he insisted.. ^_^”

Poke It

Meanwhile, I prepared the sail, using a piece of colorful paper and a straw. I threaded the paper onto the straw, pushed the straw down into the cardboard, and tadaaaa… our boat was ready :)!

Ready to Sail

In the final step, the little guy declared himself to be the captain for the afternoon and got himself busy blowing the boat… :P
