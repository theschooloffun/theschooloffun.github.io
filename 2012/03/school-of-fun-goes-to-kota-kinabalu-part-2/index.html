School of Fun Goes to Kota Kinabalu! (Part 2)

Mar 23, 2012

When you visit a place like Sabah, it’s hard not to do anything nature related. We might be skipping all the national parks this time, but it didn’t mean we didn’t get anywhere near the forest at all.

On our second day, we visited Mari Mari Cultural Village. A small van took us on a 25-minute ride away from the city. We were then grouped into small teams (1 guide per team).

The friendly guide first explained about the village, the Sabahan ethnic communities as well as the do’s and don’ts. Basically, he explained, there are 5 traditional houses representing the main 5 ethnic communities - Kadazan-Dusun, Bajau, Murut, Rungus and Lundayeh. In each house, there would be about 2 to 3 hands-on demonstrations that are unique to each tribe. Sounds very educational, eh ;) ?!

Our guide then brought us to a suspension bridge which would bring us to these houses.

Shake Shake Bridge!

The little guy couldn’t help but to squeal with joy seeing his favorite “Shake-Shake Bridge” of Thomas the Tank Engine fame in real life… :P

House Ahead!

In the next 2 hours, we learned quite a long of things, from bamboo cooking to wine making. The little guy’s most favorite part? The traditional bamboo dance performance! :)

The Bamboo Dance

My husband gave it a try and it did look fun! I really wish I could give it a try too… ^_^”

Geee… seriously, throughout this trip, he got to try out all the fun things while I waited at the sidelines… :S

Oh well, at least I got to try this delicious traditional dish… _yum, yum_
