Bloom 'n Grow!

Apr 23, 2012

Often, I found myself cringing when I read how a parent (typically, a new parent) claimed that having a kid makes him (literally) stop and smell the roses. I do agree that we, parents, really have to stop and smell the roses. Otherwise, how else would we encourage our kids to appreciate and explore the nature? Still, don’t you find it such a difficult task once the little one hits the walking stage?! At least, I do! Most of the time, I am in a race to outrun this little tornado… ^_^”

But, hey, of course, behind the phrase, there’s this message that time passes quickly and we, therefore, should cherish those precious moments with our little tornadoes. Yup, even if that means waiting for flowers to bloom… :P

No, no… don’t worry… I’m not talking about real flowers. Though it does sound like a great idea, I have no intention to let the little guy spend hours outside, watching a flower bud turn into a magnificent flower.

So, instead, we spent one morning preparing some paper flowers using smooth-surfaced paper (almost like wax-coated).

Paper Flowers

We folded the petals to the center…

Fold 'n Dip

… and put the flowers into the water with the petals facing up. Slowly but surely, each petal began to open up, just like a flower blossoming! That was indeed one great exercise for the little guy to learn how to be a patient explorer… ;)

Waiting to Bloom

We spent the next few minutes throwing in more flowers, before we decided to make some peek-a-boo faces, too.

Peek-a-boo

And once again, I’m spared from explaining about the capillary action, the idea behind the blooming flowers… ;) At least until he is much older, the little guy would be far more interested in making waves than listening to my physics lecture… :P
