Superhero Overload

May 15, 2012

Suddenly, we find ourselves having lots of wooden clothes pegs.

They surely make great musical instruments, as the little guy found out when he started his very own percussion play concert by banging away these pegs.

Still, they look… well, plain.

Plain Jane

So we thought we should put some colors on.

All we need was a little bit of red.

Red Paint

… and a little bit of blue…

Blue Paint

But then, ample, uninterrupted time to work on the detail changed everything … ;) I turned the pegs into something else while the little guy was fast asleep… :P

The Iron Man

Captain America

For the next few days, these two heroes became the little guy’s favorite companions. Everywhere he went, he made sure he brought them along. And I made sure he brought them home… hee hee… For days, they offered instant entertainment on the go for both us as we made a couple of silly stories with them. Never mind even if our Iron Man looks more like a red ninja… or if our Captain America looks like a clown in blue tights… they are still the superheroes to the little guy… :)

Geee… now I feel like making the Incredible Hulk, too… :P

Anyway, on a side note, the little guy has made the definition of a “superhero” less complicated. Apparently, in his dictionary, you don’t need to wear those tights or have superpower to be one.

In one of those recent awesome moments, as I held his hand when crossing roads together, he gave me his sweetest smile and told me, “Thank you, mommy, for stopping the bus for me…”

And that’s it! He has certainly made his mommy feel like one of those superheroes! Awww… :’)
