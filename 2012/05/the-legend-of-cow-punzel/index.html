The Legend of Cow-punzel

May 4, 2012

I’m pretty sure you’ve heard about the long-haired Rapunzel who spent her entire life in a tower. But, I bet you’ve never heard about Cow-punzel before!

‘Twas the morning before the little guy’s weekly playdate. I had to prepare for the craft activity, so I sent the little guy to play blocks on his own.

One of the Many

He gladly agreed. He let me finish in no time and so I let him take part in the pilot project… :P

Leaving His Fingerprints...

Basically, he only needed to stamp his fingerprints to make the cow’s spots. We only have blue ink at home, so our cow was kinda… uhm, funky in blue. Hee hee… :P

Next, I showed him how to put 2 clothes peg as the cow’s feet.

He might not be that interested in stamping his fingerprints (just count the number of fingerprints he made!), but he surely was interested in shutting the cow away in his block towers… :P

And so the legendary Cow-punzel went from one tower to another, never setting its feet on the barn again. For a while I was tempted to make an extendable tail for the cow… :P You know, so that we could go on like:

_Cow-punzel, Cow-punzel…
let down your tail,
so that we may climb the golden stair… _
