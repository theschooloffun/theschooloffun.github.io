Lanterns in Our Hands

Feb 7, 2013

Chinese New Year is next week and yup, of course, no matter how over-scheduled I am _yeah, right!_, I keep having the urge to join in the annual spring-cleaning.

Wanna guess what I found during my recent attempt to spring clean? A stack of red envelopes!

After the usual to-keep-or-to-throw-that-is-the-question, I decided to give some to the big brother to play with… :)

What we had:

  1. Red envelopes

  2. Stapler (or glue)

  3. String

Let the Fun Begin!

First, we folded the envelope in half.

Fold...

The big brother then helped me cut some lines along the fold line.

Snip... Snip...

Once he finished, we unfolded the envelope and stapled the edges together.

Staple here and there...

We tied a string around and… our lantern is ready!

Tadaa...

I made one myself, a slightly different version, and I had this brilliant idea of putting some lanterns along the string to hang in the hallway. Imagine, we could watch them twirl with the breeze!

Twirl... Twirl...

The big brother, on the other hand, had his own idea… :P

It's a kite!

He ran around the house, pulling the lantern behind him, as if it was a kite.

Did the poor paper lantern survive the blow?

Surprisingly, yes.

Then, along came the little brother.

The paper bag soon turned into a crumpled paper ball. A wet one… :P

The end.

^_^”

Happy Chinese New Year!

Note: And the urge to spring clean finally subsided after we made the lanterns… _phewww_…. _grinz_
