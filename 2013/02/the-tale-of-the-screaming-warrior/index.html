The Tale of the Screaming Warrior

Feb 22, 2013

So what if I’ve spent the whole morning trying to catch that little creepy crawly (for the the uninitiated, it’s the little brother!) as he tries to explore the bathroom? And the shoe cabinet? And the garbage bin?

So what if I’ve spent the whole morning working hard to get things done, while the big brother keeps on adding another million things?

So what if it hasn’t been a good day so far?

It still doesn’t justify that raging fury. It still doesn’t justify the yelling.

So what if yelling at our kids could be damaging? Why do we have to bother about their feelings when they don’t seem to bother about ours?

Oh, yeah.. you’re right.

Because they are just… well, children. And they simply do what they’re good at: playing around. Screaming at the top of their lungs. Running wild. And messing around…._gasp_

Me? I’m the adult. I guess I should be the one who behaves like a proper adult.

_spending the whole afternoon feeling guilty_

Oh yeah, parenting _is _hard work.

Harder than Kneading Bread

I think I need to learn to laugh more often. Yup, to laugh at those moments that are far less than perfect. To stop taking anything personally. To slow down.

Happy Family

And really, there’s no better time to start than now, this season of Lent.

Slow me down, Lord.

Ease the pounding of my heart by the quieting of my mind.

Steady my hurried pace.

_Give me, amidst the day’s confusion _

the calmness of the everlasting hills.
