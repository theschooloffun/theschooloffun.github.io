The Little Brother's Guide to Baby Toys

May 22, 2013

Now that we’re second-time parents, we certainly do act like ones. We are much more relaxed _What?! Babyproof the house? Gee.. do we really have to? Nahhh!_. Including, when it comes to toys.

Yup, we let the little brother has his say on what he likes to play with. These toys have been in his top 5 ever since he became mobile.

5. Bottle Caps

Clink! Clank! Clunk!

I know, it’s easy to fall in love with bottle caps…:P They’re colorful and they offer the right size and the right texture. The little brother loves scattering caps all over the floor and then reaping what he sows. Or what his big brother sows… _grinz_

4. Fabrics

It's raining scrap!

If only I knew it could get this entertaining to play with fabric scraps - without actually sewing them into anything - I would have let the big brother rummage through my fabric scraps and sewing boxes back then!

Tissue box and fabric scraps make a great stuff and pull toy. Still, nothing beats pulling ALL of **real **tissues from the box… >_<

More tissue?

3. Containers

Seriously, how can anyone NOT like containers?

They make a great roll around toy. And oh, put things inside and the noise is just an added bonus!

I Wheelie Love This!

They’re the best stacking toys, as demonstrated by the big brother. The tower rises… the tower falls… just enjoy the burst of giggles!

The Tin Tower

You could also check how you look from the mirror-like surface.

Mirror, Mirror on the Tin...

Things just get more fun when you have the right container.

Ball in a Hole!

And, oh, don’t forget those buckets, too!

The Robot and the Prisoner

2. Whatever the Big Brother is holding/playing

My turn!

Boys… don’t they just compete for everything? From toys … to cookies… to attention… _sigh_

Pretty, Please?!

While he may not yet have the built-in sensor which could warn him of trouble, the little brother obviously has a very good built-in sensor to detect when his brother has something fun to play with or something yummy to eat. It’s certainly tough to be his big brother!

1. Whatever is in Mommy’s hands

When was the last time I could read newspapers without having any diaper-clad butt on top of the newspapers?

I can’t remember… :S

Stealing the Headlines

I think the little brother’s built-in sensor provides high-sensitivity performance in detecting mommy and her fun activities.

I Can Help You, Really

Yup, just forget about those container, caps, etc… when mommy is around, everything she has is worth exploring!
