Roly-Poly Egg

Aug 20, 2013

Some books simply call us to sit down and create our own works of art. Elmer by David McKee is one example.

Another bonus point is that as soon as your child declares a book as one of his favorites, it gets even easier to put his enthusiasm to work… :)

Confetti Time

The big brother first recognized Kali Stileman’s Roly-Poly Egg as the book that his friend once brought to school. Call it a mild version of peer pressure; he asked me to borrow the book from the library.

At home, we probably read it a thousand times… >_< The big brother also loves to follow the dotted line with his finger as the adventure unrolls. Me? I love the bright colors! :)

Now, pass the brush, please… :)

What we had:

  1. Drawing paper (2)

  2. Glue

  3. Staple

  4. Old magazine or newspaper (preferably, colorful)

  5. Paint

  6. Paint brush

  7. Scissors

  8. Wiggly eyes

Let the Fun Begin!

We started off making the “egg” the day before. That way, we could leave our painted blue oval to dry overnight.

Blue Egg

The real fun began the following morning when we found blue and white paint blobs on a piece of paper.

Blue and White

We took a small piece of paper and placed it on top. We then used our palm to squeeze and swirl the paint around.

Splat!

The big brother got busy sticking some wiggly eyes and confetti (make your own using a hole punch and colorful paper!).

Small dots

We also put some colorful circles (from magazine paper) on our egg. I helped him draw a zigzag line across and he cut with the scissors.

Crack!

Finally, I stapled the egg and the colorful chick together… :) And we’re ready to play!
